If you've been researching digital advertising on construction sites, you've probably come across **Doka SiteLight**. It's the most visible international reference point for the concept — an Austrian formwork manufacturer that's integrated LED panels into its scaffolding tower systems.

It's a clever piece of engineering. But it's solving a different problem to DigiSite. And the difference matters.

## What Doka SiteLight Actually Is

Doka is one of the world's largest formwork and scaffolding system manufacturers, headquartered in Austria. SiteLight is their entry into construction-site media — LED panels integrated into Doka's proprietary scaffold tower structures.

The idea: when a Doka scaffold tower goes up on a building site, LED panels can be mounted within the system to create a digital advertising surface. The screens face outward, displaying advertising content while the construction work happens behind them.

On paper, it's the same concept as DigiSite. In practice, it's fundamentally different.

## The Critical Difference: Where People Actually Look

Here's the thing about construction-site advertising. The value isn't in putting a screen on a construction site. The value is in putting a screen **where people are looking**.

Doka SiteLight integrates with Doka's scaffold tower systems. These towers are typically used for **upper-level work** — facade construction, cladding, high-level finishing. The screens are often positioned at **mid-to-upper building height**, several storeys above street level.

That's great for visibility from a distance. But it's not where advertising attention is highest.

**DigiSite takes the opposite approach.** Our screens go on:

- **Scaffold faces at street level** — where pedestrians walk within metres of the screen
- **Perimeter hoardings** — at eye level, lining the pavement edge
- **Perimeter fencing** — along the boundary of the construction site, facing the street

This isn't an accident. It's based on how outdoor advertising actually works.

## The Science of Attention

Outdoor advertising effectiveness is driven by three factors:

### 1. Proximity

The closer a screen is to the viewer, the higher the engagement. A screen at pavement level, 2-3 metres from a pedestrian, commands far more attention than a screen 20 metres up a building. This is why bus shelter ads outperform many larger billboards on a per-impression basis — they're at arm's length.

DigiSite screens are positioned at **pedestrian and driver eye level**. Doka SiteLight screens are positioned at **building height**. The proximity difference is significant.

### 2. Dwell Time

Dwell time is how long someone spends looking at a screen. A pedestrian walking past a street-level hoarding on a narrowed pavement has high dwell time — the screen fills their visual field for several seconds. A driver waiting at traffic lights next to a scaffold face stares directly at it.

A screen at upper-building height? You glance up, register it, look away. Dwell time is measured in fractions of a second unless you're specifically looking for it.

### 3. Context

Street-level screens integrate with the pedestrian environment. They can display hyper-local content — the coffee shop across the road, the event at the venue next door, wayfinding information for the area. This contextual relevance increases engagement and makes the advertising more effective.

Upper-level screens are removed from the street context. They're visible but disconnected — more like a distant billboard than a street-level touchpoint.

## The Hardware Approach

There's another fundamental difference: the relationship between the screen and the scaffold.

**Doka SiteLight** is a **system product**. The LED panels are designed to integrate with Doka's proprietary scaffold tower system. That means:

- You need to be using Doka scaffold to use SiteLight
- The screen configuration is determined by the tower system
- The screens are part of the scaffolding product, not an independent media installation

**DigiSite** is **system-agnostic**. Our screens mount onto any standard scaffold using universal fittings (48.3mm tube clamps — the industry standard). Our DigiPerim units are modular cabinets that bolt onto any perimeter fence or hoarding framework. That means:

- Any scaffold company can be a DigiSite partner, regardless of what system they use
- Screen size and position are flexible, optimised for the specific site
- The screens are independent of the scaffold — they're a media installation that happens to be attached to construction infrastructure

This matters commercially. DigiSite can work with every scaffold company in the UK. SiteLight can only work with Doka scaffold users.

## The Market Approach

Doka is a **construction company** that has added a media product. DigiSite is a **media company** that works with construction infrastructure.

That distinction drives everything:

**Doka's perspective:** "We make scaffolding. We've added screens to our scaffolding. If you buy our scaffolding, you can have screens too."

**DigiSite's perspective:** "There are millions of pounds of untapped advertising value on construction sites. We'll work with any construction company, any scaffold system, any site — because the opportunity is in the advertising, not in the scaffolding."

Doka's model is product-led. DigiSite's model is market-led. Doka is selling a construction product with an advertising feature. DigiSite is selling advertising on construction infrastructure.

For advertisers, this matters. They don't care what scaffold system the screen is attached to. They care about **location, impressions, audience, and CPM**. DigiSite's model is built around those metrics. SiteLight's model is built around Doka's product ecosystem.

## What Doka Gets Right

Credit where it's due — Doka SiteLight proves the concept. It demonstrates that:

- LED screens can be safely and effectively mounted on construction scaffold
- Construction-site advertising is commercially viable
- There is advertiser demand for this type of inventory
- The construction industry is open to media partnerships

SiteLight has been deployed on projects in Germany, Austria, and other European markets. Every deployment validates the broader idea that construction sites can — and should — be part of the DOOH landscape.

DigiSite builds on that validation with a model designed for the UK market, optimised for street-level impact, and open to the entire construction industry — not just one manufacturer's customers.

Doka SiteLight answers the question: **"How can we add more value to our scaffold system?"**

DigiSite answers the question: **"How can we unlock the advertising value of every construction site in the UK?"**

Both are valid questions. But only one of them creates a national media network.
