Every day, millions of people walk past construction sites in UK city centres. Scaffolding wraps around buildings on high streets, perimeter hoardings line busy roads, and tower cranes dominate skylines. For months or even years at a time, these structures occupy some of the most prominent, high-traffic locations in the country.

And yet, almost all of that visual real estate goes completely to waste.

## The Dead Media Problem

Traditional construction hoardings are printed panels — static, weather-beaten, and typically displaying nothing more than a developer's logo or a planning notice. Scaffold wraps, when used for advertising at all, are enormous vinyl banners that take weeks to produce, can only display a single message, and are expensive to change.

This is what we call **dead media** — physical advertising space that can't be updated, can't be targeted, and can't be measured.

In a world where every other advertising channel has gone digital, construction sites remain stuck in the analogue age.

## Enter Digital Out-of-Home (DOOH)

Digital out-of-home advertising refers to any digital screen or display used for advertising in public spaces. You'll see it in shopping centres, at bus stops, on motorway gantries, and across transport networks. The UK DOOH market is worth over **£1.5 billion** and growing at more than 10% year on year.

DOOH solves the fundamental limitations of static media:

- **Dynamic content** — Ads can be changed in seconds, not weeks
- **Dayparting** — Different messages at different times of day
- **Programmatic buying** — Advertisers bid for slots in real time
- **Measurable impressions** — Footfall and traffic data drive reporting
- **Multiple advertisers** — One screen can serve dozens of campaigns per day

The question isn't whether DOOH works — it's proven. The question is: **why hasn't it reached construction sites yet?**

## Why Construction Sites Are the Perfect DOOH Opportunity

Construction scaffolding and perimeter hoardings have characteristics that make them uniquely suited to digital advertising:

### Prime Locations

Construction happens where the demand is — city centres, high streets, transport hubs, regeneration zones. These are exactly the locations that advertisers pay premium rates to reach.

### Extended Duration

A typical scaffolding project lasts 6 to 18 months. That's 6 to 18 months of guaranteed, high-visibility advertising inventory in a fixed location. Compare that to mobile billboards or temporary event signage.

### Captive Audiences

Pedestrians walking past a scaffold on a narrow pavement have nowhere else to look. Drivers stopped at traffic lights beside a hoarding are a captive audience. The dwell time and attention metrics for construction-adjacent advertising are exceptional.

### Untapped Supply

There are over **160,000 active construction sites** across the UK at any given time. Even capturing a small fraction of these represents an enormous volume of new advertising inventory that simply doesn't exist today.

## How It Works in Practice

The concept is straightforward: replace static hoardings and scaffold wraps with commercial-grade LED screens. These screens are:

- **High-brightness** (3,000+ nits) — clearly visible in direct sunlight
- **Weather-rated** — IP65 or higher, built for UK conditions
- **Remotely managed** — content updated via cloud-based CMS
- **Modular** — can be configured to fit different scaffold and hoarding dimensions

The screens integrate with the existing scaffold or hoarding structure, powered from the site's temporary supply. Content is managed centrally, and advertising is sold programmatically — meaning brands can buy impressions on construction-site screens just as easily as they buy digital ads anywhere else.

## The Business Model

The revenue model creates value for everyone in the chain:

- **Construction companies** earn income from scaffolding that previously generated nothing
- **Site owners and developers** see their hoarding become a revenue stream rather than a cost
- **Advertisers** gain access to premium urban locations at competitive CPM rates
- **Local authorities** benefit from better-maintained, well-lit construction boundaries

It's a model where the infrastructure already exists — scaffolding goes up regardless. The digital layer simply transforms a cost centre into a revenue opportunity.

## What's Next
