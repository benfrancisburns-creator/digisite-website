The UK's out-of-home advertising market hit **£1.46 billion** in 2025, with digital formats now accounting for around 64% of total OOH revenue. Meanwhile, UK construction output continues to grow, with major regeneration programmes underway in almost every major city.

These two trends are on a collision course — and the result is one of the most compelling media opportunities in years.

## The Numbers Tell the Story

Consider the scale of the opportunity:

- **160,000+** active construction sites across the UK at any given time
- **£1.46bn** UK OOH market, growing at 10%+ annually
- **64%** of OOH spend now goes to digital formats
- **6-18 months** average duration of urban scaffolding projects
- **Zero** dedicated platforms connecting construction sites to DOOH networks

The infrastructure is already in place. Scaffolding goes up on high streets, arterial roads, and city centres every single day. It stays there for months. And right now, that visual real estate generates nothing.

## Why Now?

Three converging factors make this the right moment:

### 1. Technology Has Matured

Commercial LED displays have dropped dramatically in cost while increasing in performance. Screens rated at 3,000+ nits — bright enough to be clearly visible in direct sunlight — are now commercially viable at scale. Cloud-based content management systems like Samsung MagicINFO allow remote scheduling and real-time content updates from anywhere.

Five years ago, the hardware wasn't ready. Now it is.

### 2. Programmatic DOOH Has Arrived

The advertising industry has spent the last decade building the infrastructure for programmatic digital out-of-home buying. Platforms like Vistar Media, Hivestack, and The Trade Desk now allow advertisers to buy DOOH impressions in real time, targeted by location, time, audience, and context.

This means new screen inventory — including construction-site screens — can be monetised from day one through existing demand-side platforms. There's no need to build a sales force or sell directly to brands. The buying infrastructure already exists.

### 3. Construction Companies Need New Revenue

The construction industry operates on thin margins. Scaffold hire, in particular, is a competitive, cost-sensitive business. The ability to generate advertising revenue from scaffold structures that are already being erected transforms the economics of a project.

For scaffold companies, this isn't a distraction from their core business — it's a new revenue layer on top of work they're already doing.

## Comparing the Opportunity

To understand the value, compare scaffold-mounted digital screens to existing DOOH formats:

**Traditional roadside billboards** — Prime 48-sheet or 96-sheet digital billboards in city centres command CPMs of £8-15. But available locations are limited, planning permission is difficult, and new installations face significant opposition.

**Bus shelter and street furniture** — High-frequency but small format. CPMs are lower (£3-6), and the screens compete with hundreds of other street-level distractions.

**Scaffold-mounted screens** — Large format (comparable to 48-sheet), prime urban locations, elevated positioning for maximum visibility, 6-18 month guaranteed availability. And because they're temporary structures attached to existing construction projects, they bypass many of the planning barriers that limit traditional billboard expansion.

The gap in the market is clear: **large-format, premium-location, digitally-enabled screens that can be deployed wherever construction is happening** — which, in UK cities, is everywhere.

## Who Benefits?

The scaffold advertising model creates a value chain that works for every participant:

- **Scaffold companies** earn a revenue share on advertising sold through their structures — turning a cost-only asset into a profit centre
- **Developers and site owners** can offset project costs with advertising income, or improve the visual impact of their construction boundary
- **Advertisers** gain access to large-format digital screens in locations they couldn't previously buy — often directly outside retail, transport, and entertainment venues
- **Local communities** see better-maintained, well-lit construction boundaries instead of faded, damaged hoardings
- **Local authorities** benefit from improved streetscapes around construction zones

## The Competitive Landscape

The traditional OOH sector is dominated by established players — JCDecaux, Clear Channel, Global, and Ocean Outdoor. These companies control the majority of permanent digital screens across the UK. But they operate a fundamentally different model: long-term leases on fixed locations.

Construction-site advertising is a different category entirely. The inventory is **temporary by nature**, tied to the lifecycle of a building project. It requires a different operational model: relationships with scaffold companies, rapid deployment and removal, integration with construction programmes, and compliance with site-specific regulations.

This isn't a market that existing OOH operators are set up to serve. It requires a purpose-built platform.

## The Path Forward

The opportunity is real, the technology is ready, and the market is waiting. What's needed is the operational layer — the company that can bridge the construction industry and the advertising industry, handling everything from scaffold integration to programmatic ad serving.

That bridge is being built right now.
