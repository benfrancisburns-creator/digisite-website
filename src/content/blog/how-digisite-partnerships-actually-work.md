We get it. When someone says "no cost to you, just passive income," your guard goes up. It sounds like one of those LinkedIn pitches that ends with a £4,000 course.

So let's be completely transparent about how DigiSite partnerships work. No jargon. No small print surprises. Just the actual mechanics.

## What You Have That We Need

You have **physical structures in high-traffic urban locations**. Scaffolding on a high street. Hoardings around a development site. Perimeter fencing at an event venue. A boundary wall on a busy road.

These structures are visible to thousands of people every day. In the advertising industry, that visibility is worth money — real, measurable money based on footfall data and industry-standard pricing.

Right now, that money is going to waste. You're not capturing it. Neither is anyone else.

## What We Bring

DigiSite brings everything needed to turn that visibility into revenue:

- **The screens** — commercial-grade LED displays, rated for outdoor UK conditions
- **The installation** — our team fits and removes the screens, working around your programme
- **The content management** — cloud-based CMS handles all advertising content remotely
- **The advertising sales** — we sell the screen time through programmatic DOOH platforms
- **The compliance** — we handle planning consent, advertising regulations, and all legal requirements
- **The insurance** — our installations are fully insured
- **The maintenance** — if a screen needs attention, that's on us

You don't buy anything. You don't sell anything. You don't manage anything.

## What You Get

A **share of the advertising revenue** generated by screens on your sites.

The exact percentage depends on your partnership type and the specifics of the agreement. But the structure is simple:

1. Screens go on your site
2. Advertising is sold
3. Revenue comes in
4. You receive your agreed share
5. When the scaffold comes down (or the event ends), the screens are removed

The revenue share is paid regularly, and you get full transparency on what's been earned. No black-box accounting. No "we'll tell you later." Clear numbers, clearly reported.

## What You Give

**Permission.** That's it.

You allow DigiSite to install screens on your construction infrastructure for the duration of the project. You provide access for installation and removal. And you continue doing exactly what you were already doing — building, developing, hosting events.

Your scaffold programme doesn't change. Your construction timeline doesn't change. Your operations don't change. A screen gets attached to a structure you were building anyway, and it starts earning money.

## The Genuine Concerns (And Honest Answers)

### "Will it delay my build programme?"

No. Screen installation is designed to work within your existing programme. Installation typically takes a few hours, not days. And it's scheduled around your works, not the other way around.

### "What if my client doesn't want advertising on their building?"

Your client gets a share of the revenue too — or can use screen time for their own marketing. A developer selling apartments can display CGIs and pricing on a screen that's bigger and brighter than any hoarding wrap. Most clients see this as an upgrade, not a compromise.

### "What about planning permission?"

DigiSite handles all advertising consent applications. The partnership agreement is conditional on consent being granted — if consent is refused, no screens go up and nobody owes anything. You carry zero planning risk.

### "What if the screen gets damaged on site?"

DigiSite's screens are fully insured. If something happens — accidental damage, weather, theft — that's our problem to resolve, not yours.

### "What's the minimum commitment?"

There's no long-term lock-in. Partnerships are structured around individual sites or events. If you want to try one site first, that's fine. If it works and you want to roll it out across your portfolio, even better.

### "Why would you give us money for doing nothing?"

Because you're not doing nothing. You're providing the most valuable thing in advertising: **location**. Your scaffolding is in places advertisers want to be. You own the structure. You control the access. That has value — you've just never been paid for it before.

You didn't buy the screen. You didn't sell any advertising. You didn't manage any content. You just let us attach something to a structure you were building anyway.

## Why This Model Works

DigiSite can offer this because the economics of digital advertising are strong enough to support a revenue share. A screen in a good location generates significantly more in advertising revenue than it costs to install and operate. The surplus is what gets shared with partners.

It's the same model that makes shopping centre advertising work — the landlord provides the location, the media company provides the screens and sells the advertising, and both share the revenue. DigiSite is simply applying that proven model to a new category of location: construction infrastructure.

There's no charity involved. No subsidy. No venture capital being burned on below-cost deals. The advertising revenue genuinely supports the model — and everyone in the chain gets paid.
