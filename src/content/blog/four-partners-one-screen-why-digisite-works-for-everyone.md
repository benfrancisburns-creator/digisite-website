Most new business models work by taking a slice from someone else's pie. Uber took from taxi firms. Airbnb took from hotels. Spotify took from record shops.

DigiSite doesn't take from anyone. It creates a pie that didn't exist before — and gives a slice to everyone involved.

That's not marketing speak. It's structural. Here's how.

## The Pie That Doesn't Exist Yet

Right now, a scaffold face on a UK high street generates **zero advertising revenue**. A perimeter hoarding around a development site generates **zero**. The construction company earns nothing from it. The developer earns nothing. The council earns nothing. The local community gets nothing.

It's not that someone else is earning that revenue instead. **Nobody is.** The value simply doesn't exist in the current system.

DigiSite creates that value by placing digital screens on construction infrastructure and selling advertising. The revenue that comes in is new money — it wasn't being earned by anyone before. And because it's new, sharing it doesn't mean taking it from someone else.

That's the foundation. Now let's look at who gets what.

## Partner 1: Construction & Development Companies

**What they have:** Scaffolding and hoardings on active construction sites in prime urban locations.

**What they get:**
- A **revenue share** from advertising sold on their sites — passive income on infrastructure they were building anyway
- **Zero upfront cost** — DigiSite funds, installs, and manages the screens
- **On-site power infrastructure management** — DigiSite's power setup for the screens often benefits the wider construction site
- **Improved site appearance** — a well-lit digital screen looks better than bare scaffold or torn hoardings

**What they give up:** Nothing. The scaffold goes up the same way. The build programme doesn't change. They simply allow a screen to be attached to a structure they already own.

This is the anchor partnership. Without construction companies, there's no inventory. That's why DigiSite's model is designed to make the construction partner's involvement as frictionless as possible — no cost, no operational burden, no risk.

## Partner 2: Landlords & Landowners

**What they have:** Property or land where construction is happening, or vacant sites, boundary walls, and prominent frontages.

**What they get:**
- A **revenue share** from screens on their property — turning dead frontage or a construction-phase building into an income stream
- **Developer branding on screen** — CGIs, marketing, pricing for new developments shown alongside commercial advertising
- **Enhanced security** — well-lit, monitored screens deter vandalism and fly-tipping
- **No capital expenditure** — DigiSite handles everything

**What they give up:** Permission to install screens on their property boundary. That's it.

For landlords with long-term vacant sites — land banking, awaiting planning, between tenants — this is particularly powerful. A site that's costing money in maintenance and security suddenly starts generating income.

## Partner 3: Commercial & Public Sector

**What they have:** Civic infrastructure, public realm frontages, council-owned development sites, universities, hospitals, transport corridors.

**What they get:**
- **Advertising revenue** from screens on public infrastructure
- **Community content slots** — public health messaging, event promotion, wayfinding, emergency information displayed alongside commercial advertising at no cost
- **Improved public realm** — bright, maintained screens replacing dead hoardings around council-led development
- **Social value delivery** — measurable community benefit that supports planning and procurement requirements

**What they give up:** Agreement to host screens on public-realm construction boundaries.

This is the partnership that excites local authorities. The ability to display community messaging on screens that pay for themselves — rather than spending public money on temporary information boards — is a genuine efficiency gain.

## Partner 4: Events

**What they have:** Temporary event sites — festivals, concerts, sporting events, markets, exhibitions — with perimeter fencing and high-footfall audiences.

**What they get:**
- **Revenue from event-site advertising** — brands pay premium rates to reach engaged, targeted event audiences
- **Rapid deployment** — DigiPerim is designed for fast install and removal, fitting tight event setup windows
- **Sponsor integration** — event sponsors' content runs alongside commercial advertising
- **Production value** — digital screens elevate the look and feel of event boundaries

**What they give up:** Permission to install screens on event perimeter fencing for the duration of the event.

Event partnerships are high-intensity, short-duration — a weekend festival, a two-week sporting event. The CPM rates are higher because the audience is premium. And for event organisers, it's a new revenue line that offsets event costs.

## The Fifth Beneficiary: Advertisers

Partners aren't the only ones who win. The advertising brands buying screen time get something they can't buy anywhere else:

- **Large-format digital screens in locations that don't currently exist** as DOOH inventory
- **Hyper-local targeting** — a screen on a specific street, in a specific neighbourhood, reaching a defined audience
- **Contextual opportunities** — advertising a new restaurant next to the building site where it's being built. Promoting a development to the pedestrians walking past it every day
- **Flexible booking** — programmatic buying means they can book a single day or a full quarter

For media agencies and brands, DigiSite inventory fills a gap in the DOOH landscape. It's not competing with existing screens — it's adding new ones in places that have never had them.

## The Sixth Beneficiary: Communities

This one matters. Construction sites disrupt communities — narrowed pavements, noise, dust, visual impact. DigiSite's model can give something back:

- **Better-lit streets** around construction sites, improving pedestrian safety
- **Community messaging** on screens — local events, council notices, emergency information
- **Revenue for community projects** through structured community benefit programmes
- **A more attractive construction boundary** than bare plywood and torn mesh

Nobody likes living next to a building site. But a building site that lights the street, displays useful information, and funds a local youth club? That's a different conversation.

## Why "Everyone Wins" Actually Works

The reason this model is viable — not just aspirational — is that it's built on **new value, not redistributed value**.

Traditional business models often create winners and losers. Someone's margin gets squeezed. Someone's market share gets taken. There's a finite amount of money, and the new entrant is fighting for a share of it.

DigiSite's model is different because the underlying asset — advertising revenue from construction infrastructure — **genuinely doesn't exist today**. The screens aren't competing with existing billboards for advertising spend. They're creating new inventory in new locations, funded by new revenue that nobody was earning before.

When the pie is new, everyone gets a slice without anyone losing theirs.
