## 1. WHY THIS SECTION EXISTS — AND WHAT IT IS NOT

Earlier planning referred to this section as "Key Partners" with the intention of displaying partner logos. That framing has been corrected.

**The reality:** DigiSite has no confirmed commercial partnerships at this stage. Displaying logos under a "Partners" heading — even with careful wording — implies commercial relationships that don't yet exist. That is a credibility risk, not a credibility boost. Sophisticated investors will ask about those relationships. The answer "we've spoken to them" does not survive that question.

**What this section actually is:** A demonstration that DigiSite has selected and built its model on established, reputable, proven infrastructure — hardware, data platforms, CMS, and programmatic marketplace technology that already exists and already works at scale. DigiSite is not asking investors to fund technology development. The screens exist. The CMS exists. The audience data exists. The programmatic marketplace exists. DigiSite is the operator that connects all of it to a new inventory type.

**Framing decision — two options:**

**Option A — "Built on proven technology"**
- Presents hardware suppliers and platforms as the technological foundation DigiSite runs on
- Logos displayed with brief role description — no implication of commercial partnership
- Copy angle: "We didn't build the screens. We built the business that deploys them."
- Risk: still implies more formalised relationships than exist. Any sophisticated reader will note that Hikvision, Route and Vistar are companies DigiSite intends to work with, not companies DigiSite has agreements with.

**Option B — Remove this section entirely from Phase 1 site**
- The site makes its case through the business model, market data, and operational credibility
- No logos that imply relationships DigiSite doesn't have
- Add this section in Phase 2 once real partner agreements are signed
- Replace with a stronger version of the Roadmap section, or expand the Market section

**Recommendation: Option B for Phase 1.** The site is stronger without it than with a version that risks being questioned. When DigiSite has its first signed scaffold partner, that name goes here and it means something. A logo grid of companies DigiSite intends to use hardware from is not the same thing.

**If Option A is preferred**, the full specification below applies.

## 2. SECTION CONCEPT (OPTION A)

The section presents three categories of proven technology infrastructure under a single honest framing:

- **Screens** — the hardware DigiSite has specified and received proposals for
- **Content & Data** — the CMS and audience measurement platform DigiSite will operate on
- **Marketplace** — the programmatic advertising infrastructure DigiSite will list inventory through

No logos. No partner names. Just clear, confident descriptions of the proven infrastructure DigiSite is built on. If logos are used, they are presented as technology used, not partners — and the section heading and intro copy must make that unambiguous.

**Section anchor:** `id="technology"`

## 6. WHAT THIS SECTION DOES NOT DO

- Does not display partner logos
- Does not use the word "partner" for any company listed
- Does not imply commercial agreements exist where they don't
- Does not list Samsung as a hardware partner (MagicINFO is their CMS software, not a hardware partnership)
- Does not reference OpenMedia or OpenSpace anywhere
- Does not reference Brand Access Solutions / Lyndon SGB (they belong in the Partnerships scheme section as a target partner type, not here)

This section can evolve into a genuine partner showcase once DigiSite has signed agreements. The update is simple: replace the three-column technology grid with a partner grid, add logos, update the heading from "technology" to "partners," and add a brief role description per partner.

The first signed scaffold company goes here. That name is worth more than a grid of aspirational logos.
