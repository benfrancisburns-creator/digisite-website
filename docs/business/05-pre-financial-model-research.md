## PART 1 — RIGGING SYSTEM: WHAT WE NOW KNOW

### The Concept: Tube + Key Clamp Frame System

The DigiSite rigging concept — using scaffold tube (48.3mm OD) with custom-designed connectors at 90° on the same axis — aligns directly with an established UK product category called **Key Clamp fittings** (also known as tube clamps, Q-Clamps, Kee Klamps, Interclamps).

These are hot-dip galvanised malleable iron fittings that bolt onto 48.3mm tube using Allen key grub screws. No welding required. Available from multiple UK suppliers off-the-shelf (Scaffolding Supplies, DC Iron, Key Clamps Store, etc.).

**Standard fitting types available:**
- 90° elbow — connects two tubes at right angles
- Short Tee / Long Tee — T-junction connections
- 3-way corner / 4-way cross — multi-tube junctions
- Wall/floor flanges — fixing to surfaces
- Swivel fittings — variable angle connections

**Unit pricing (approximate, ex-VAT, UK market):**
- 90° elbow: ~£4–6 each
- Tee fitting: ~£4–7 each
- 3-way corner: ~£5–7 each
- Scaffold tube (48.3mm, per metre): ~£3–5

This system is well-proven in the UK for guardrails, display frames, event rigs, lighting structures and signage stands. There is a very important caveat however (see below).

### Critical Distinction — Key Clamps vs Scaffold Couplers

**Key clamp suppliers explicitly state:** the 48.3mm key clamp system should NOT be used for constructing a scaffolding framework itself. Key clamps are for secondary structures — handrails, frames, fixtures.

The **connection between DigiSite's tube frame and the scaffold structure** must use proper **scaffold couplers** (right-angle couplers, swivel couplers, putlog couplers) manufactured to BS EN 74. These are the standard scaffold fittings used by the scaffold industry.

**In practice, the DigiScaff rig would work as two interlocking systems:**
1. **The sub-frame** — built from 48.3mm tube and key clamp fittings. This forms the rectangular grid that the screen cabinets bolt onto.
2. **The scaffold connection** — using proper EN74 scaffold couplers, the sub-frame tubes attach to the existing scaffold standards (uprights) and ledgers (horizontals) at multiple pre-determined points.

This approach means:
- The sub-frame is a **standardised, reusable, kit-of-parts** — manufactured once, assembled and disassembled on each site
- No welding on site — purely bolted assembly with Allen keys and scaffold spanners
- The structural engineer's job is to specify: how many scaffold attachment points, what type of couplers at those points, what tube sizes in the sub-frame, and how screen cabinets fasten to it

### Custom Components — What Needs to Be Made

The one element that does need custom manufacture (or careful specification) is the **bracket that connects the LED cabinet rear mounting points to the 48.3mm sub-frame tube**. Hikvision's cabinets have standard mounting slots/points on the rear; a fabricated steel clamp/bracket bridging those to the tube would need to be designed and batch-manufactured. This is low-complexity metalwork — any fabrication shop can produce these once the design is finalised.

### Screen Perimeter Bar — Canvas Integration Solution

The key clamp/tube system also solves the canvas attachment question. The sub-frame's outer perimeter (the rectangular border around the screen array) forms a 48.3mm tube border that the canvas wrap can tension directly against — using bungee cord through eyelets or pole pockets — which is **exactly how the existing scaffold wrap industry attaches large canvas prints to frames**.

This means:
- **No need for a separate bespoke screen border product** — the tube frame IS the border
- Canvas is supplied by standard scaffold wrap/building wrap suppliers, cut to fit with eyelets on the perimeter
- Bungee cord or lashing tensioned from canvas eyelets to the perimeter tube provides a taut, professional finish
- A small gap between screen face and canvas edge (~50–100mm) would allow front-access maintenance without removing the canvas; alternatively, canvas can be removed quickly for maintenance and re-attached

**Canvas material specification:**
- 330gsm PVC mesh (allows wind to pass through — recommended for structural load management)
- OR 500–720gsm solid PVC (denser, better weather protection, but higher wind loading — structural engineer input needed)
- M1 fire resistant material preferred (some planning authorities will require this)
- Hemmed edges with reinforced eyelets, printed to brand/sponsor specification

### Recommended Next Steps — Rigging

- [ ] Finalise sub-frame layout drawings (screen array dimensions → tube grid positions)
- [ ] Identify fabricator for custom cabinet-to-tube brackets
- [ ] Determine scaffold attachment point design (right-angle coupler to sub-frame tube, clamped to scaffold standard/ledger)
- [ ] Submit drawings to structural engineer for formal calculations and sign-off

## PART 2 — SCREEN SIZES: STANDARD CONFIGURATIONS

Before building the financial model, DigiSite needs to settle on **two or three standard screen configurations** that will be used across sites. This avoids one-off pricing and enables repeatable cost modelling.

### DigiScaff Proposed Standard Sizes

The Hikvision DS-D4257GO-2AAA uses 960×960mm cabinets. Sensible standard configurations for scaffold installations:

| Config | Grid | Total Size | Area | Approx Weight |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Small | 3 wide × 2 tall (6 cabs) | 2.88m × 1.92m | 5.5 m² | ~167kg |
| Medium | 6 wide × 3 tall (18 cabs) | 5.76m × 2.88m | 16.6 m² | ~500kg |
| Large (standard) | 9 wide × 4 tall (36 cabs) | 8.64m × 3.84m | 33.2 m² | ~1,001kg |

The "large" is the Hikvision-specified standard configuration. The "small" suits narrower scaffold frontages or lower lift heights. The "medium" is a mid-range option for wider sites.

**Recommendation:** Settle on Small and Large as the two standard DigiScaff products. Medium can be offered on request. This keeps manufacturing, transport, and installation consistent.

### DigiPerim Proposed Standard Sizes

The Dynamo DVO800d uses 960×960mm cabinets at ~25kg/m². Perimeter screens suit lower, wider formats. Proposed standards:

| Config | Grid | Total Size | Area |
|---|---|---|---|
| Single bay | 3 wide × 1 tall (3 cabs) | 2.88m × 0.96m | 2.8 m² |
| Double bay | 6 wide × 1 tall (6 cabs) | 5.76m × 0.96m | 5.5 m² |
| Standard panel | 3 wide × 2 tall (6 cabs) | 2.88m × 1.92m | 5.5 m² |

For perimeter fence use, single-row (landscape, low-height) units suit most fencing applications. The standard panel suits slightly higher perimeter walls.

## PART 4 — REVENUE BENCHMARKS: WHAT RESEARCH SHOWS

### How UK OOH Advertising Is Actually Bought and Priced

The standard UK outdoor advertising booking unit is a **fortnight (two weeks)**, not a week. Most campaigns are priced and sold per two-week cycle — confirmed by all major industry sources. Some operators quote weekly rates but the minimum booking is almost always two weeks, so the real minimum spend is always double the weekly figure.

**Real-world UK rates per advertiser per fortnight (verified, December 2025–January 2026):**

| Format / Location | Rate Per Advertiser Per Fortnight |
|---|---|
| Local/regional roadside static | £300–£800 |
| Suburban digital, outer city | £800–£2,000 |
| Manchester city centre digital | £1,000–£4,000 |
| Birmingham / Leeds / Liverpool digital | £800–£3,000 |
| London outer boroughs digital | £800–£2,000 |
| London prime digital (Shoreditch, Waterloo) | £5,000–£15,000+ |
| Piccadilly Circus / Oxford Street | £30,000–£100,000+/month |

Sources: media.co.uk, exciteooh.com, 75media — January 2026.

**Share of Voice model — how DigiSite earns revenue:**
DOOH digital screens rotate multiple advertisers in a 60-second loop. Typical: 6 advertiser slots, each getting ~10 seconds. Each advertiser pays per fortnight for their slot. DigiSite sells those slots directly.

**Full utilisation gross revenue — Manchester city centre, 6-slot rotation:**
- 6 x £1,000/fortnight = **£6,000/fortnight → £156,000/year** (conservative)
- 6 x £2,500/fortnight = **£15,000/fortnight → £390,000/year** (mid market)
- 6 x £4,000/fortnight = **£24,000/fortnight → £624,000/year** (premium)

**Programmatic CPM (fills unsold inventory):**
~£10–£20 per 1,000 impressions. Lower yield than direct sales but automatic — fills gaps without sales effort.

### DigiSite Revenue Model — Corrected Working Assumptions (Per Screen, Manchester City Centre)

| Scenario | Slots Sold (of 6) | Rate per Slot | Gross per Fortnight | Gross Annual |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Conservative (new, ramp-up) | 2 | £1,000 | £2,000 | £52,000 |
| Base case (established) | 3 | £1,500 | £4,500 | £117,000 |
| Optimistic (strong site) | 5 | £2,500 | £12,500 | £325,000 |

**These are gross revenues before: partner revenue share (10–20%), SSP fees (15–20% of programmatic), operational costs and tax.**

**Pilot pricing strategy — new entrant positioning:**
Price below the Clear Channel/JCDecaux rate card initially. Suggested pilot rate: £800–£1,500 per slot per fortnight. This attracts advertisers while reputation builds. Rates increase as the network grows and Route audience data validates impressions.

## PART 6 — WHAT CAN BE MODELLED NOW VS WHAT MUST WAIT

### Can model now (with estimated/researched figures):
- Capital cost ranges (hardware + installation + planning per screen)
- Revenue sensitivity analysis (price per slot × occupancy × number of screens)
- Operating cost structure (fixed overheads)
- Break-even analysis (how many screens at what utilisation rate)
- Investment requirement ranges

### Must wait for formal quotes:
- Accurate per-screen capital cost (Hikvision and Dynamo pricing needed)
- Accurate ongoing power cost (DNO tariff per site needed)
- Accurate insurance cost (broker quotes needed)

### Approach for financial model:
Build a **scenario-based model** with clear input assumptions flagged as estimated/confirmed. Three scenarios: conservative, base case, optimistic. Once formal quotes arrive, update input cells and the model recalculates automatically. This lets DigiSite present to investors with honest caveats while showing robust financial logic.
