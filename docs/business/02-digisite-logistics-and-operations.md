## 2. STORAGE & DEPOT

### Requirements
The DigiSite depot needs to fulfil four functions:
1. **Storage** — secure, dry, temperature-stable environment for screens and hardware
2. **Workshop** — space to prep, test, assemble and pack equipment before deployment
3. **Logistics hub** — level or bay access for van loading/unloading with tail lift
4. **Asset management** — organised racking/shelving for inventory tracking

### Specification
- **Size:** 1,500–2,500 sq ft (pilot phase); expandable as inventory grows
- **Location:** Within 20–30 minutes of Liverpool city centre — Speke, Knowsley, Aintree, or the Baltic Triangle/Edge Lane industrial corridors are all viable options with good commercial unit availability
- **Power:** 3-phase supply preferred — enables screen testing before deployment
- **Security:** Alarmed, CCTV-monitored (requirement for equipment insurance — screens are high-value assets)
- **Access:** Level access or loading bay to accommodate a Luton van with tail lift
- **Estimated cost:** £800–£1,500/month (Merseyside industrial, units of this size)

### What Gets Stored
- LED screen panels in flight cases (fragile, high value — prioritise careful racking)
- DigiScaff rigging frames (steel/aluminium tubes and clamps — robust but bulky)
- DigiPerim cupboard units (flat-pack or assembled)
- Lucy Zodion feeder pillars
- Cable drums and cabling runs
- Fixings, consumables and spares
- Tools, PPE and harnesses
- Replacement/spare LED panels and modules
- Internet connectivity hardware (routers, antennas)

### Asset Management
Every item must be uniquely tagged and tracked from day one:
- Unique asset ID per screen, per rigging frame, per feeder pillar
- Inventory management system (even a simple spreadsheet initially, scaling to dedicated software)
- Status tracking: In storage / Deployed (site name + date) / In maintenance / Written off
- Feeds directly into: insurance schedules, depreciation accounting, deployment planning

### Phase 2 Storage
As DigiSite expands into multiple cities, options include:
- Small satellite storage units in each city (e.g. Manchester, Leeds)
- Central Liverpool/Merseyside hub with overnight accommodation budgeted for crews on distant sites
- Partnership with construction logistics firms for shared storage/yard access

## 3. VEHICLES

### Fleet Strategy — Phase 1 (Pilot)
**Lease rather than buy** for all vehicles initially. Known monthly cost, manufacturer warranty cover, no large capital outlay, flexible at end of term.

### Vehicle 1 — Luton Box Van (Primary Workhorse)
- **Type:** 3.5-tonne Luton box van with tail lift
- **Purpose:** Transport screen panels (in flight cases), rigging components, cable, Lucy Zodion pillars, tools, PPE and two crew to/from site
- **Why Luton:** Box body = dry, secure, clean space for fragile/expensive screens. Tail lift = safe loading/unloading of heavy flight cases. 3.5t = standard driving licence. Two crew in cab.
- **Fitout:** Internal racking/shelving for organised load; tie-down anchor points; non-slip floor matting
- **Recommended models:** Ford Transit Luton, Mercedes Sprinter Luton, VW Crafter Luton
- **Estimated lease cost:** £400–£600/month (3-year lease)

### Vehicle 2 — Management/Survey Vehicle
- **Type:** Mid-range SUV or estate
- **Purpose:** Founder use — site surveys, partner meetings, investor presentations, planning authority meetings; also carries survey equipment, laptop, presentation materials
- **Recommended models:** VW Golf Estate, Skoda Octavia Scout, Ford Kuga, Hyundai Tucson
- **Estimated lease cost:** £350–£500/month (3-year lease, company vehicle)

### DigiPerim Framework Transport
Framework sections (likely 2–3m lengths) may exceed Luton van internal dimensions. Options:
- **Roof rack / roof bars** on van (for lighter aluminium sections)
- **Plant trailer** (3.5m, approx £1,500–£2,000 to purchase outright — most flexible option)
- **Haulage subcontractor** for first delivery to each site (when framework installed, it stays until the site ends)

### What DigiSite Does NOT Need to Own
- HGV / flatbed lorry — hire or subcontract per job
- Cherry picker / MEWP — **hire per job** (see section 4)
- Forklift — use depot provider's forklift, or hire as needed

## 4. WORKING AT HEIGHT — EQUIPMENT STRATEGY

### DigiScaff Installations
The key insight: because screens are being installed *onto existing scaffold*, the scaffold itself is the working platform. Access is via the site's own internal ladder frames or stair towers. Screen panels and rigging components are carried up manually or via the site hoist (coordinated with the scaffold contractor).

This means DigiScaff installs generally **do not require a hired MEWP**. Instead, the installation crew needs:
- **PASMA certification** (Prefabricated Access Suppliers' and Manufacturers' Association) — for working from scaffold and mobile tower platforms
- **Working at height training** — harness use, risk assessment, exclusion zones
- Coordination with the principal contractor for site access, permit-to-work, induction

### DigiPerim Installations
Perimeter framework installation is predominantly at or near ground level (1.5–3m). A MEWP is generally not required. Small podium steps or a low-level mobile platform are sufficient for reaching the top of the framework.

### When a MEWP IS Required
- Pre-installation survey/access assessment on a scaffold face where internal access isn't available
- Maintenance visits where scaffold internal access has changed or been restricted
- Specific DigiScaff installs where the site hoist isn't operational or screen position requires outreach access

### MEWP Recommendation — Hire Per Job, Do Not Buy
- **Type needed:** Articulating boom lift (cherry picker) — ideal for facade access, can reach over obstacles, 360° swivel base, suitable for outdoor uneven ground
- **Typical working height needed:** 6–20m depending on site (most initial sites likely 8–15m)
- **Power type:** Diesel for outdoor construction sites (electric/hybrid for noise-sensitive or city centre sites)
- **Hire cost:** Approximately £250–£500/day from local plant hire companies
- **Liverpool/Merseyside-area providers:** Sunbelt Rentals, Hewden, HSS, Nationwide Platforms, Nationwide Access
- **IPAF certification required:** Operators must hold IPAF 3b (mobile boom) PAL card — ensure all installation staff are certified

### Certifications Required (Installation Crew)
- IPAF 3b — Mobile boom lift operator (PAL card, valid 5 years)
- PASMA — Mobile scaffold tower operator
- CSCS card — Construction Skills Certification Scheme (required for most construction sites)
- Working at Height awareness training
- Manual handling training (lifting heavy screen panels)
- First aid (at least one crew member)
- Harness + fall arrest training

## 6. MOBILISATION PROCESS — SITE DEPLOYMENT SEQUENCE

### Pre-Deployment (weeks/months in advance — long lead items first)
1. Partnership agreement signed with site owner/contractor
2. Site survey — physical assessment, dimensions, screen placement, power access, internet signal
3. Structural engineering assessment commissioned — load calculations, wind analysis, sign-off
4. Advertisement consent application submitted to local planning authority
5. Heritage statement commissioned and submitted (if required — conservation area, listed building setting)
6. DNO connection application submitted to local electricity supplier (long lead time — up to 12+ weeks — apply early)
7. Internet connectivity provisioned — rugged outdoor router ordered/configured
8. Hardware allocated from stock inventory (or ordered if stock insufficient)
9. Planning permission received — DO NOT mobilise without this
10. DNO connection confirmed and scheduled

### Deployment Week
11. Lucy Zodion feeder pillar delivered and installed by qualified electrician
12. Rigging frames/DigiPerim framework delivered and installed (coordinated with scaffold contractor or perimeter team)
13. Screens transported to site in Luton van (flight cases, tail lift)
14. Screen panels installed, cabled, and secured to rigging
15. Internet router installed and connectivity tested
16. Power connection commissioned
17. CMS configured — screens added to network, content loaded
18. Full system test — all panels, brightness settings, remote monitoring active
19. Sign-off and commissioning document completed
20. **Go live**

### Ongoing Operations
- Remote monitoring via CMS (daily) — screen health, content status, connectivity, brightness
- Scheduled maintenance visits (monthly recommended minimum)
- Reactive maintenance within agreed SLA (target: 24-hour response to screen failure)
- Content management — advertiser uploads, scheduling, proof-of-play reports
- Power and connectivity billing reconciliation with site contractor
- Planning condition compliance monitoring (operating hours, brightness limits)
- Screen relocation/removal coordinated when scaffold is struck or site ends
